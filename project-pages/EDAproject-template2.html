Problem Statement:
A private equity firm, has purchased an airways and plans to expand into the domestic Indian market. To succeed in this venture, they need insights into the factors influencing airline pricing, competitor pricing strategies, and the key drivers of price variations. This analysis is crucial for setting competitive yet profitable pricing strategies in a dynamic market.
Specifically, the project aims to explore the following questions:
üí∏1) Compare pricing across competitors
üõ´2) Understand the factors that influence airline pricing

Project Summary:
1) Data Preparation: Cleaned and merged datasets of 79,635 flight details and corresponding price trends to ensure consistency. Segmented flights by class (Economy, Premium Economy, Business).
2) Exploratory Data Analysis: Examined pricing trends based on: Booking Time (Relationship between fare and days before the journey); Departure Time (Variance in pricing based on flight times); Flight Duration (Correlation between flight length and fare); Seasonality (Monthly demand patterns); Destination Popularity (Identified high-demand routes).
3) Competitor Pricing Analysis: Benchmarked Majestic Airways against key competitors (e.g., Vistara, Air India); Compared average fares and market share by airline.
4) Model Development: Built a regression model to determine factors most strongly influencing price increases.
Python Code (using Numpy, Pandas, Seaborn, Matplotlib)
Inferences and Conclusion:
Air India and Vistara command the highest average fares, may be due to their tiered service offerings.
Vistara leads with 51.3% share, followed by Air India at 29.65%, with smaller players occupying the remaining market.
Fares increase as the departure date approaches due to last-minute demand.
Morning flights tend to have higher fares, reflecting peak demand periods.
Longer flights incur higher costs and correspondingly higher fares.
